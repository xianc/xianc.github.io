The Design Process

I wanted to break free from the traditional one page bootstrap style layouts that many sites are fashioning these days. I wanted to create a site whoes navigation is not a dropdown menu nor a horizontal bar. So I went to photoshop and made a very basic image of how wide I want the navigation to be compared to the content body.

I chosed the colors out of whim. I went with a very dark grey and a very light grey instead of black and white. Its a habit that I developed after doing years of traditional art.

The very next step, and the most important step for me, is to choose a colour theme. This is the most important step, because colour inspires me. If I don't select a good colour combination, the layout design ends up falling flat. I just need to find one colour that inspires me, and I can work from there.
I decided that I want a vibrant colour and went on the internet and search for "flat colors" for inspiration. One of the first search results I got was this. And I was immediately drawn to the Persian Green.

From there, I started building on my wireframe. It turns out, I really like the original background colours against the persian green, so I kept it and just worked with some composition and styling for the links.

At this point, I just need to add some small finishing touches before jumping into the code. I found some nice flat icons created by Jorge Calvo on Google and added to the bottom of the navigation menu. I noticed that the content column is lacking some green, so I layered a persian green dotted background over the light grey.
